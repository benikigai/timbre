# 1st place at Rosewood Hospitality 2030 hackathon

One of my favorite moments in life is meeting someone chasing the purpose they were made for. Someone living so deep inside their ikigai. That rare alignment of love, mastery, contribution, and craft. You feel it within seconds. The air around them sings. You're jolted awake. There's a frequency that radiates off them. An aliveness, a sense of freedom, and this inexplicable joy. Once you've felt it, you can't un-want it. You want it for them. You want it for yourself. And you want to spend the rest of your life chasing it down.

Hackathons are the closest vehicle I've found to chase this moment. This weekend, Cerebral Valley brought together Rosewood Hotel Group, Anthropic, ElevenLabs, and Greycroft for the Hospitality 2030 hackathon at Rosewood Sand Hill. A space that makes you exhale before you've thought about why. A room full of builders is the closest thing to alchemy I've found.

Builders who'd flown in from Mexico City, from Canada, from halfway across the country just for this. Founders so deep in their work they spoke a language outside what I know. Yet I listened, fascinated anyway. Operators running short-term rentals, wanting to compare notes. Everyone fully alive in their purpose and I got to live inside it for 12 hours.

Before I build, I research. Sponsors, brand, tools, problem space. Even if it costs 1/3 of my build time. That's how I found the quote.

> "All our hotels are individual, but A Sense of Place is the red thread that runs throughout them all."
> — Sonia Cheng, CEO of Rosewood

The name and the thesis at once.

The setting shaped this build more than any hackathon before. It was impossible to default to the usual AI aesthetic; dark mode, neon accents. Rosewood doesn't speak that language. So I spent real time on the brand. Cream and ink. Red Chinese endless knot 紅線, the thread of fate. Brand and craft were part of the build, not bolted on after the prototype worked.

The focus on design, brand, and tech paid off. RedThread won the Hospitality 2030 hackathon!

Thank you to Cerebral Valley for the room, Rosewood Hotels for hosting, Anthropic, ElevenLabs, and Greycroft for the tools and opportunity. To every builder I met. The air around you was singing. Keep going.
